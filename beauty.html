The Real Costs of Fake Beauty

1 in 3 consumers would not buy from a brand again after purchasing a counterfeit

Cautious but Vulnerable

Nearly half of all consumers report being more cautious about buying from a brand after a counterfeit experience. Brands suffer while counterfeiters exploit their credibility, often beyond their direct control.

Loyalty at Risk

Nearly 1 in 3 consumers wouldn’t buy from a brand again after buying a counterfeit bearing its name, while a quarter of all users trust the brand less. Even when it’s not their fault, brands pay the price, losing valuable customer loyalty and confidence.

Word-of-Mouth Damage

Negative experiences have a lasting impact—26.2% of consumers warn others about a brand they perceive to be associated with counterfeits, often on social media. These warnings create a ripple effect by deterring future customers.

Growing Expectation for Brand Protection

15.8% of consumers blame brands for not removing counterfeits. Brands are held responsible for ensuring authentic experiences,
underscoring the need for robust anti-counterfeiting measures that secure customer trust and loyalty.
